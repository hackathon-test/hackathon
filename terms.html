1. General Provisions

1.1. IBA Hackathon is a competition that gathers programmers with different skill sets to work collaboratively, on a software project, which creatively solves various problems on a given topic.

1.2. The purpose and objectives of the Hackathon:

  • Identify disruptive ideas that could help to develop use cases in targeted areas;
  • Search for new solutions and technologies with possible further implementation in the Organizer's business activities (by separate agreement with the participant); 
  • Develop a culture of innovation and IT ecosystem of Azerbaijan in general.
  • Encouraging young professionals who want to challenge themselves, show their vision and offer new approaches;
  • The popularization of modern technologies.

1.3. Organizers of the Hackathon

International Bank of Azerbaijan (IBA) – it is a National Development Bank, providing important support to the stability of Azerbaijan banking system. It is a universal bank applying most advanced technologies to provide the wide range of high quality services to the customers.

Allocation of the Hackathon Organizer: 67 Nizami str., Baku, Azerbaijan.

4. The order of the Hackathon

5.1. Location of the Hackathon – Baku Expo Center, 515 1050, Heydər Əliyev prospekti, Baku.

5.2. The Hackathon will take place in several stages described below:

/ Stage 1: Forming stage. Participants will form Teams and start to work the development of their projects along with Mentors.

/ Stage 2: Semi-final. Teams will pitch developed projects in front of the Mentors, projects will be estimated and preselected for the next stage.

/ Stage 3: The final. From 10 to 15 preselected Teams will pitch their projects in front of Judges.

‍

5.3. Stage 1: Forming stage. 

5.3.1. Applicants can participate either with beforehand formed Team, either individually.

Participants will form Teams of two (2) to four (4) Participants, to focus around a single project of developing a prototype. A Participant cannot be a part of more than one Team. 

5.3.2. Teams will be given time for ideation, development of their prototype or pretotype.

5.3.2. Teams could get professional expertise and advice from Mentors during scheduled Mentors’ session, and by scheduled beforehand Mentors’ individual consultation.

‍

5.4. Stage 2: Semi-final.

5.4.1. Organizers will provide a structure, according to which Teams will present their developments  on the pitching.

5.4.2. All participating Teams will pitch prototypes of project/solutions to the Mentors. After presentations of the prototypes, Mentors will have a closed vote. As a result of a closed vote, from10 to 15 prototypes will be selected for the next stage of the Hackathon.

‍

5.5. Stage 3: The final.

5.5.1. Teams of selected projects have to improve the prototype and prepare for the final pitch, in accordance with the requirements of the Organizers.

5.5.2. Selected Teams will pitch prototypes of project/solutions to the Judges. After presentations of the prototypes, Judges and Mentors will evaluate projects and will have a vote. 

5.5.3. Participants will be able to vote for any project other than their own. Votes of participants will form 12,5% from total score during the projects evaluations by Judges.

5.5.4. Judges will determine and announce the Winners of the Hackathon. 

5.5.5. Judges’ decisions are final and cannot be challenged.

‍

5. Evaluation criteria

Judges will evaluate projects based on the following criteria. Each criterion has a scale from 1 to 5.

  • Appropriateness to the theme and business value. Does the project address the challenge? Resolving the existing issues? Does the solution created for a significant number of customers? How big the market is?
  • Originality and Innovations. The uniqueness of the idea. How the proposed solution is better than current solutions?
  • User experience and functionality. Is the solution customer-oriented? How user-friendly the program/app is?
  • Technical complexity. How complex and sufficient the project is?
